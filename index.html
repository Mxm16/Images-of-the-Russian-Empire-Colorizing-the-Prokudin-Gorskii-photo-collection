Images of the Russian Empire: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii photo collection

Overview

In this project, I implemented an algorithm to align the RGB channels of Prokudin-Gorskii’s glass plate images to reconstruct color photos. The algorithm works on both small .jpg images and large .tif images.

Approach

My method was divided into three steps:

  • Channel Separation: Each glass plate image was split into three parts, corresponding to the blue, green, and red channels.
  • Alignment:
    • For small images, I shifted one channel within ±15 pixels in both directions, computed the SSD with the reference channel, and chose the shift with the lowest error.
    • For large images, I used pyramid alignment: the image was repeatedly downsampled (by 0.5) until it was small, aligned at that level, and the shift was scaled back up. At the full resolution, the coarse shift was applied and refined with a small local search (±5 pixels).
  • Post-processing: After alignment, the three channels were stacked into a color image. Pixel values were clipped to [0,1] and then converted to uint8 for saving.
